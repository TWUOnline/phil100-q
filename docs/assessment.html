Assessment

The following assignments are opportunities for learners to demonstrate their understanding of the course outcomes. Please confirm assignment details with your instructor.

Note that Assignment dropboxes are located in the Moodle Assessment tab. Also refer to the Course Schedule in Moodle for the specific due dates.

Assignment

Grading Criteria

The following rubric explains how your assignment will be evaluated.

Unit 1 Reflection Assignment Grading Rubric (20%)

  1. Provide detailed examples from your life in the reflections (1-5 points)
  2. Integrate the course content with your life experiences (1-5 points)
  3. If you disagree with any of the course content, explain why and provide additional content to teach us about the topics (1-5 points)
  4. Show a degree of polish in the essay (i.e. does the essay include proper sentence structure, grammar, punctuation, etc.) It’s often easy to spot a polished essay over a last-minute essay. Write as clearly as possible. Double-check your work. Ask a friend or family member to read your reflections and offer comments. There are many tools available for improving our writing. Take advantage of these tools. (1-5 points)

Unit 1 Reflection Assignment (20%)

Write a 3-paragraph reflection on friendship.

Your reflection should include the following:

  1. Your own view of friendship in light of what you have learned. Be sure to include your experiences – both how you view yourself as a friend to others, and how you view other friends and their relationship with you.
  2. Integrate the content from unit 1. Specifically, how do Aristotle and Aquinas influence your view of friendship? If they don’t influence your view, then why and what replaces these views?

Unit 2 Reflection Assignment (20%)

Write a 3-paragraph reflection on humility.

Your reflection should include the following:

  1. Your own view of humility in light of what you have learned. Be sure to include your experiences – both how you view yourself and how you view other people.
  2. Integrate the content from unit 2. Specifically, how do you understand and apply the allegory of the knowledge rooms and the reflection on Socrates’ being the wisest man? If these ideas do not influence your view of intellectual humility, then why and what replaces these views?

Unit 3 Cumulative Final Assignment (60%)

Write a 750-word reflection on what you learned in the course. Be sure to include:

  1. Your experiences with detailed examples for each topic.
  2. An integration of the course content with your answers. If the course content did not help you in any way, then explain how the course content is either mistaken or unhelpful. What content would be more appropriate for teaching us about these three topics?
  3. A strategy moving forward. What is your plan to improve yourself in each of these three topics? Specifically, what is your plan to improve your own friendship quality with others and what is your plan when you seek out current and/or new friends? What is your plan for demonstrating intellectual humility? If you disagree that such a thing should be practiced, why do you think that and what support do you have for your view? What is your plan for improving your rhetoric? What types of goals are you going to set for your next encounter?

A reminder: Integrate the course content as much as possible; again, if you disagree with the course content, then you must provide justification for that disagreement and replace the content with something else. Use specific examples from your life experience with details. The more details you can provide about your experiences with the integration of the course content, the higher the grade.
